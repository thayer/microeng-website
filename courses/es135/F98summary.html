ENGS 135: Thin Films and Microfabrication Technology

ORC description:

This course covers the processing aspects of semiconductor and thin film devices. Growth methods, metallization, doping, insulator deposition, patterning, and analysis are covered. There are two major projects associated with the course -- an experimental investigation performed in an area related to the student's research or interests, and a written and oral report on an area of thin film or microfabrication technology. Prerequisite: ENGS 24 or equivalent. Gibson and Levey.

Lecture Subjects:

Examples (introduced at the beginning and threaded through the course)

  • MOSFET device

  • MEMS device

  • Optical bandpass filter

  • Magnetic sensor

Bulk/substrate growth techniques
  • single crystals - melt, LPE, MOCVD
Device design, layout, modeling

Device fabrication

  • Substrate Preparation

  • Ion Implantation

  • Film growth techniques -insulators, metals, sputtering, evaporation,

    oxidation/nitriding, CVD deposition

  • Vacuum processing (vapor pressure, substrate, dirt levels, equipment etc.)

  • MOCVD

  • MBE

  • Patterning - photoresist, e-beam, x-ray lithography

  • Etching (wet and dry)

Characterization techniques

alphabet soup - in vacuo and out, substrate prep, data format & interpretation, electical (Hall, capacitance, C-V, ellipsometry, ...)

Grading

  • Midterm:30%
    Covers the lecture material on the fundamentals
  • Homework: 10%
  • Lab project: 40%
    Formal report on the design, fabrication and performance of a device made in the lab., plus a 15-20 minute oral report given in class.

  • Literature research paper: 20%

    paper on a topic suggested or approved by the instructor.
