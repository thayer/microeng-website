There are several weekly news bulletins of relevance to the physics community. Bob Park's What's New is a spicy editorial of relevant events in Washington; Phillip Schewe's Physics News Update (and PNU back issues) is a brief review of physics research announcements; the OSA publishes their own Washington Watch; the AIP's frequent FYI news releases summarize science policy developments in Washington affecting the physics and astronomy community; and for physics educators there is the biweekly Physics Educator's News: PEN which summarizes information on resources, national initiatives, outreach programs, grants, professional development opportunities, and publications related to physics and science education.

Personal

Primary interests: hiking, biking, backpacking, skiing ( current backcountry conditions at Tuckerman Ravine on Mt. Washington NH; snow conditions for VT, NH, Conway NH (audio)), dance, and folk music. I teach social and ritual English Dance traditions: my English Country Dance series runs in the winter and spring, and I am the dance coach for a teenage young men's morris team, the Green Mountain Morris. I have performed as a member of Oak Apple Morris (Madison WI), Millstone River Morris (Princeton NJ), and Four Corners Morris (Thetford, VT). I was dance choreographer for the recent Revels North production of a Celtic Christmas Revels. Our pets include a Border Collie and two cats; our lawnmowers (PHOTOS) are two Navaho Churro sheep and two Angora goats.
