Albert K. Henning

NOTE: Al Henning has taken a new position in the MEMS field, at Redwood Microsystems in Menlo Park, CA as of 1 January 1996. Watch this space for new, Redwood-related Web pages.

Al Henning is Adjunct Associate Professor of Engineering. From 1987 through 1995, he was Assistant, and then Associate, Professor in both the Engineering Sciences Department at Dartmouth College, and in the Thayer School of Engineering at Dartmouth. He founded, and directed, the Solid State Microengineering Laboratory at Thayer School, a Class 100/1000 cleanroom facility for microelectronics and micro-machines (devices and materials) research and teaching.

Research Areas and Interests

  • Areas: semiconductor device physics; semiconductor characterization techniques and measurements; bridging technology computer aided design (TCAD) and device fabrication (FAB) through device characterization, and model verification and calibration; process technology and device development for microelectromechanical systems (MEMS).
  • Interests: measurement of two-dimensional dopant profiles in semiconductors using scanning Kelvin probe microscopy (SKPM); failure analysis of microelectronic devices and processes using SKPM; silicon transport modeling using Monte Carlo methods; verification of silicon mobility models by comparing Monte Carlo simulations with mobility measurements; SiGe MOSFET design, characterization, and modeling; thermocouple MOSFETs and related embedded sensors for smart maintenance applications (description of National Nanofabriation Facility project); novel approaches to silicon wafer bonding; control of out-of-plane, three-dimensional structures using thin-film stress engineering; novel micro-fluidic structures; micro-scale magnetic and electric field sensors; electromechanical pressure and viscosity sensors.

Teaching Areas and Interests

  • Areas: Semiconductor device physics; microelectronic and MEMS device fabrication technology; engineering systems (ordinary differential equations applied to anything and everything).
  • Interests: Development of systems and databases for dynamic publication of scientific and technological 'textbook' information (DARTEXT) -- with especial application to engineering systems, and MEMS; undergraduate course and curriculum development in MEMS; development of Laplace transform visualization techniques for engineering systems.
