Workshop Facilities

The Solid State Microengineering Laboratory at Dartmouth College, completed in 1990, is a Class 100/1000 (lithography/general processing) clean room. The lab is used for teaching and research in microelectronic and micromachine fabrication, at both the graduate and undergraduate levels. The SSL is directed by Professor Albert Henning and is managed full-time by Dr. Christopher Levey.

The Microelectronics Center of North Carolina (MCNC) and Analog Devices, Inc. have recently been funded by the federal government to serve as microelectromechanical systems (MEMS) foundries. Each accepts CAD designs from U.S. companies and universities. The designs are fabricated and then returned to the originators for evaluation. The workshop will make use of these facilities for the final student design projects. Once fabricated, these projects will be returned to students at their home institutions for evaluation.

UWE (Unix Workstation Environment) is a campus-wide, centrally administered network of over 250 engineering workstations for high-powered computing, design, and analysis.
