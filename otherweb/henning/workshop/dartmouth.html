About the campus ...

Thayer School emphasizes engineering fundamentals and student creativity: the hands-on laboratory courses stress individual design and experimentation. Students also benefit from accessible professors and a low student:faculty ratio (~12:1).

Dartmouth College is located in Hanover, New Hampshire, along the Connecticut River and astride the Appalachian Trail. It is a small university, with 4000 undergraduates, and 1000 graduate students in business, engineering, medicine, music, and the sciences. Undergraduates attend classes during their sophomore summer, creating an active campus year-round. The libraries hold over 2 million volumes. Dartmouth pioneered the use of computers in the undergraduate curriculum, and the Kiewit Computation Center remains the hub of such activity.

Recreational facilities include running tracks, tennis courts, an Olympic-size pool, and facilities for basketball, gymnastics, dance, squash, racquetball, fencing, riflery, volleyball, crew, and, of course, weight-training. Outdoors, the Connecticut River offers kayaking, swimming, and canoeing. There are also unlimited opportunities locally for hiking and mountain biking.

The Hopkins Center and the Hood Museum offer a broad range of cultural and social activities including theater, dance, music, films, and lectures. The Hood houses Dartmouth's fine art collections as well as traveling exhibits.
