Personnel

Albert K. Henning, the workshop director, is an associate professor with the Thayer School of Engineering. He was responsible for the planning, design, and construction of the Solid State Microfabrication Laboratory (SSL) and is the director of the lab. A 1977 graduate of Dartmouth, Professor Henning worked as a device physicist at Intel Corporation in California. He holds a Ph.D. in electrical engineering from Stanford University.

Christopher G. Levey, manager of the SSL, is also an adjunct professor of physics. He has been teaching physics at Dartmouth since 1986, and currently teaches microfabrication lab sections of courses utilizing the SSL. Dr. Levey has worked in research at AT&T Bell Labs and at Bellcore. He received his Ph.D. in physics from the University of Wisconsin at Madison.

Several Dartmouth undergraduates will participate in the workshop and will provide peer information on Dartmouth, Thayer School, and the Hanover, N.H. environs.
