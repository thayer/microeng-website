Liftoff Process  (optimized for LOR 1A 2um spaces)

Modified: 02/11/2005

I    -   Organic Clean

  1. Methanol rinse (1 min)
  2. Ethanol rinse (brief)
  3. DI rinse (brief)
  4. N2 blow dry.

II   -     LOR - S1813 Deposition

  1. Dehydrate substrate:  5 min @ 200 deg. C.(hotplate)
  2. Spin HMDS:         5 s. @ 900 rpm, 60 s. @ 4000 rpm. (1)
  3. Spin LOR 1A:       5 s. @ 400 rpm, 45 s. @ 3000 rpm (for 1250 A of LOR) (2)
  4. LOR Prebake:      1 min @ 110 deg. C (hotplate)
  5. Spin Photoresist - S1813:    5 s. @ 900 rpm,  60 s. @ 4000 rpm. (1)
  6. EBR can be applied here (difficult - many defects) - process
  7. S1813 Prebake:    2 min @ 110 deg. C (hotplate)
  8. Expose:     6s  (optimal per 2/5/2005 - possible range 4s - 9s)
  9. Puddle Develop:    LDD-26 for 15 s. (in petri-dish, important: keep a constant flow through wafer surface)
  10. Double rinse in DI:    (dip in petri-dish to stop develop., flow rinse over spinner)
  11. N2 blow dry.
  12. Clean the bowl with EBR - PG (to avoid clogging)

IId - Self-Aligning Etch (optional)

  1. Etch in 10:1 BHF for 11.5s to get ~ 300 A cut-in
  2. Triple DI rinse.

III    -    Metallization

  1. Pump down to 1 x10-6 Torr.
  2. Evaporate Cr-adhesion layer (50 A - rate 2A/s.)
  3. Evaporate Au-layer (250-600 A, rate 3A/s.)

IV    -    Metal Lift-off

  1. Bath I: PG Remover - Ultrasonicate:    30 min. (water in basin heated up to 60 deg. C prior to liftoff)
  2. Bath II: PG Remover:                          30 min. @ 30-40 deg. C.
  3. IPA rinse (brief)
  4. DI water rinse (brief)
  5. Dehydrate bake: 5 min. @ 110 deg. C. (hotplate)

IIb    -    Resist Reuse (if LOR or S1813 fails)

  1. Remover PG - periodic agitation -     5 min.
  2. IPA rinse (brief)
  3. DI rinse (brief)
  4. N2 blow-dry
  5. Dehydrate 5 min. @ 110 deg. C.

IIc    -    Edge Bead Removal Process (low yield - many defects due to splashing) (3)

  1. 10 s. ramp, 3s. @ 1200 rpm, dispense EBR for <1s (squirt) at edge of wafer
  2. 2s ramp, 3s @1700 rpm
  3. 2s ramp, 10s @ 2500 rpm
  4. 10s ramp-down
